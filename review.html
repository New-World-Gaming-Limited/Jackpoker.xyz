About JackPoker

JackPoker launched in 2021 under the parent company Jack La International Limitada. It started life as a poker room focused on Brazil and Latin America — markets that were underserved by the major networks — and has since been expanding globally with English, German, Spanish, Russian, and Portuguese interfaces.

The room holds a license from the Government of the Autonomous Island of Anjouan, Union of Comoros, which is a legitimate (if smaller) offshore jurisdiction used by a number of online gaming operators. More meaningfully, JackPoker is independently certified by iTech Labs, BMM TestLabs, and GLI — three respected testing labs that verify RNG fairness and software integrity. That's a more thorough certification stack than plenty of larger rooms carry.

At any given time you'll find around 1,000 players online. That's not PokerStars numbers, obviously, but it keeps the games liquid at the micro and low stakes, which is where most recreational players are anyway. The room's whole philosophy is built around making poker fun again — there are active anti-toxic policies, and the bonus structure is clearly designed to reward players for showing up, not just for being a rakeback grinder.

Software & Platform Good

JackPoker runs on a browser-based web client that works in any modern browser on any operating system — Windows, macOS, Linux, ChromeOS, it doesn't matter. You open the site, log in, and you're at the tables. No client download, no update pop-ups, no installation headaches.

Mobile is handled two ways. Android users get a dedicated APK you download directly from the JackPoker site (it's not in the Play Store). iOS users get a Progressive Web App — open the site in Safari, tap "Add to Home Screen," and it runs like a native app. It's not quite as slick as a proper App Store app, but it works well for cash game sessions.

What the Lobby Looks Like

The lobby has multi-stage filters so you can quickly zero in on the game type, stakes, and number of players you want. You can preview a table before sitting down, which is handy for checking the average pot size or whether the table has fish. The interface is clean and doesn't feel cluttered.

Table Features

Once you're seated, the table experience is solid. The key features worth knowing about:

  • Multi-tabling: Up to 3 tables open at once. Not great for mass multi-tablers, but fine for recreational players.
  • Enhanced bet sliders: Quick-bet buttons are customizable, which cuts down on fumbling around with the slider.
  • Player notes: You can tag and note opponents across sessions — these carry over between tables.
  • Streaming mode: Hides your cards and stack size for screen-sharing or streaming without leaking info.
  • Run it twice: Available on all cash game tables. Both players need to agree.
  • Game settings: You can customize table felt colors, card backs, and a few other visual preferences.

The main thing missing is a dedicated desktop client. If you prefer the feel of a downloadable app or you rely on third-party HUD software, JackPoker's browser-based setup won't satisfy you. For everyone else, it's more than adequate.
