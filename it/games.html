PLO4 — Four Card Omaha

The original and most widely played Omaha variant. Each player receives four hole cards and must use exactly two of them combined with exactly three community cards to make their best five-card hand. PLO4 rewards players with strong equity assessment skills and an understanding of blockers, redraws, and nut potential — skills that transfer directly from tournament Omaha into our cash game tables.

Our PLO4 stakes run from the entry-level PLO10 ($0.05/$0.10) all the way to PLO500 ($2.50/$5), with the most active tables at PLO25, PLO50, and PLO100. Six-max is the dominant format, though 9-max tables run at selected lower stakes for players who prefer a tighter, more patient style.

5
High Action

PLO5 — Five Card Omaha

Five-card Omaha amplifies every aspect of the PLO4 experience. With an additional hole card, hand equities run closer together pre-flop, post-flop play becomes more complex, and the frequency of premium hands increases dramatically. This means bigger pots, more multi-way action, and far fewer situations where a single player can dominate with a straightforward nut draw.

PLO5 at JackPoker has quickly gained a dedicated following among players who want more action per hand and who thrive in the complex equity landscapes that five hole cards create. Stakes range from PLO10 through PLO200, with peak traffic during European prime time. If you're moving up from PLO4, PLO5 will challenge your hand-reading and equity realization skills in entirely new ways.

PLO6 — Six Card Omaha

PLO6 is one of JackPoker's most distinctive offerings — a format you'll find at very few online poker rooms worldwide. Six hole cards per player creates an almost chaotic pre-flop equity landscape where virtually every starting hand can connect powerfully with the board. Monster hands are far more common, and the game rewards players who can accurately assess their relative hand strength in spots where the nuts are often spread across multiple players at once.

This variant is not for the faint of heart — PLO6 generates enormous variance even for strong players, and bankroll management at these tables requires particular discipline. That said, it's also where recreational players feel most comfortable, because every hand feels like a live draw. JackPoker offers PLO6 from PLO10 through PLO100, making it accessible to players at almost every bankroll level while still offering meaningful stakes for experienced Omaha specialists.

Push-Fold

The ultimate test of short-stack poker theory. Every decision is binary: push all your chips in or fold. No betting, no limping, no complex multi-street decisions — just the pure mathematics of expected value.

Push-Fold is a specialized poker format that strips the game back to its most fundamental decision point: is my hand strong enough to shove all-in from this stack depth, at this position, against these opponents? With every player starting with a fixed short stack — typically between 5 and 15 big blinds — every action from the moment cards are dealt is either a push all-in or a fold. There is no pre-flop raise, no call, no check. The simplicity is deceptive: played correctly, Push-Fold demands rigorous knowledge of GTO shoving ranges, opponent modeling, and ICM pressure.

For players serious about improving their poker fundamentals, Push-Fold at JackPoker is one of the best training tools available. Playing hundreds of Push-Fold hands in an evening burns short-stack ranges into muscle memory in a way that traditional cash games or tournaments simply cannot replicate at the same speed. You'll quickly develop an intuitive understanding of which hands are profitable shoves from each position, and how those ranges adjust as stack sizes change and blinds increase.

Push-Fold is also a genuinely enjoyable format for casual sessions. Giochi resolve quickly — a full Push-Fold table typically plays out within five to eight minuti — making it ideal for players who want real poker action without committing to a lengthy cash game grind. The fast pace means you experience a high concentration of interesting spots in a short timeframe, and the swings are naturally managed by the binary structure of the game. All that said, the format rewards knowledge: players who have studied push-fold charts consistently outperform those relying purely on feel, making it one of the few poker variants where studied preparation pays off almost immediately.

Our Push-Fold game is available across multiple stake levels, from micro-stakes entry points where new players can learn the format at minimal risk, up through mid-stakes tables where serious players compete for meaningful prize pools. The speed of play also means your hourly rake contribution accumulates quickly, helping build your VIP rakeback earnings at a rate that would take many more ore to achieve at conventional cash game tables.
